## SECTION: SIMULATIONS DEEP DIVE

### Layout
Left: Step-by-step content
Right: Visual — product demo, animation, or video placeholder

> **Note to dev:** This section needs a strong visual. Ideally a short looping video or interactive animation showing a candidate inside a simulation — making a decision, seeing the scenario respond. This is the single highest-conversion element on the page. Even a stylized UI mockup is better than text alone.

### Headline
Watch candidates perform — not just answer.

### Body
Cognitive simulations drop candidates into realistic work scenarios. Not hypotheticals. Not case studies. Live decisions with consequences, in a workspace that feels like their first week on the job.

### Steps

**1. You describe the role. We build the scenario.**
Upload a job description. The system analyzes the cognitive demands of the role — crisis management, stakeholder navigation, analytical rigor, creative judgment — and generates a simulation matched to what the job actually requires.

**2. The candidate enters a realistic challenge.**
They see a workspace with dashboards, documents, data, and a problem to solve. An SRE candidate faces a system losing stability. A product manager walks into conflicting stakeholder priorities. It feels like real work because it's modeled on how real work unfolds.

**3. Every decision has consequences.**
Rush a fix without investigating? The situation might get worse. Overcommit to a stakeholder to keep them happy? That promise compounds. The simulation responds to choices the way the real job does — no hidden answer key, no trick questions.

**4. The challenge adapts when we need to see more.**
If the baseline scenario can't separate strong from exceptional, the stakes rise — a second failure hits, a constraint tightens, a relationship breaks down. Not random difficulty. A calibrated test of how candidates adapt and recover.

**5. You get a clear recommendation — not a vague score.**
What the candidate did. How they approached risk. Whether they investigated before acting or guessed. How they performed when pressure increased. Every finding maps to a specific action they took.

# IMPLEMENTATION NOTES FOR CODING AGENT

### Visual priorities (highest impact first)
1. **Simulation demo visual** — The #simulations section needs a video, animation, or interactive mockup. This is the single highest-conversion element. Even a stylized UI showing "candidate makes decision → scenario responds" is better than text alone.
2. **Logo wall** — Add below trust bar as soon as client logos are available. Use a placeholder line until then.
3. **Comparison table** — The signal strength dots (●○○○○) should be rendered as a visual indicator, not text. Consider a horizontal bar or filled dots.
4. **Use case cards** — Render as a scannable grid. Each card: role question as headline, scenario as body, measurement tags as footer pills.

### Scroll rhythm
Each section should occupy roughly one viewport height. The visitor should get a complete thought at every scroll-stop:
- Scroll 1: Hero — the insight + what QualifyMe is
- Scroll 2: Problem — why current methods fail
- Scroll 3: Comparison — simulations as the natural evolution
- Scroll 4: Platform — two tiers, clear distinction
- Scroll 5: Simulations — how it works (the centerpiece)
- Scroll 6: Use cases — "that's my role"
- Scroll 7: Results — proof it works
- Scroll 8: Pricing — what it costs
- Scroll 9: CTA — ready to act

### Launch staging
- **Now (pre-simulation launch):** Simulation sections show "Early access" tags. CTA is "Join Early Access" or "Join the waitlist." Hero visual shows screening UI.
- **Post-launch:** Change tags to "Live." Change CTAs to "Start Free" or "Try a Simulation." Update hero visual to include simulation UI. Move simulation walkthrough higher on page if conversion data supports it.

### Responsive behavior
- Problem grid: 3 columns → stacked on mobile
- Platform overview: 2 cards side by side → stacked on mobile
- Use case cards: 2×3 grid + 1 full-width → single column on mobile
- Comparison table: horizontal scroll on mobile, or convert to stacked cards
- Pricing: side by side → stacked on mobile
