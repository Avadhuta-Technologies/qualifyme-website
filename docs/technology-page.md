## SECTION 1 — Philosophy

**H2:** We separate AI assistance from AI decision-making

Most hiring tools let AI do too much. We don't.

QualifyMe uses AI where it accelerates the process — and steps it back where accuracy and accountability matter most. Every score a recruiter acts on is traceable, repeatable, and explainable.

**Three pillars (cards):**

| Pillar | Description |
|---|---|
| **Deterministic Scoring** | Same answer, same score. Always. No variance based on who reviews the application. |
| **Bounded AI** | AI assists in defined, visible ways. It doesn't rank candidates or make hire/no-hire calls. |
| **Full Explainability** | Every output ties directly back to candidate behavior on a defined assessment. |

## SECTION 2 — Platform Layers

**H2:** Three layers. One hiring signal.

**Layer 1 — Assessment Engine**

Handles how assessments are created, delivered, and captured. Supports multiple question formats — MCQs, coding problems, open-ended responses, and video Q&A. AI helps generate role-specific questions from a job description. Once the assessment is live, the question set is fixed and identical for every candidate.

**Layer 2 — Evaluation Layer**

Scores candidate responses against the assessment's pre-defined expectations. Objective question types use rule-based scoring. For open-ended and video responses, structured rubrics — defined before evaluation begins — guide AI-assisted review. Scoring criteria are set at job creation, not inferred after the fact.

**Layer 3 — Confidence Engine**

Aggregates response scores, behavioral signals, and completion patterns into the **QualifyMeScore** — a normalized, comparable score that lets you rank candidates fairly across the same role, regardless of when they applied or who was available to review them.

## SECTION 3 — AI Usage Boundaries

**H2:** Where AI helps — and where it doesn't

We publish this because trust requires transparency.

**AI is used for:**
- Generating role-specific questions from job descriptions
- Structuring evaluation rubrics for open-ended responses
- Surfacing behavioral anomalies during assessments
- Producing human-readable summaries for recruiting teams

**AI does not:**
- Score objective question types (that's rule-based)
- Rank or compare candidates without a pre-defined rubric
- Make a hire or no-hire recommendation
- Use candidate data for model training

## SECTION 4 — Scoring Methodology

**H2:** Scores your team can stand behind

The QualifyMeScore is composite — not a black box. It's built from three signals:

1. **Task Performance** — How accurately did the candidate answer what was asked, measured against defined criteria?
2. **Behavioral Consistency** — Did the candidate engage with the assessment as intended? Time-on-task, response patterns, and completion signals.
3. **Communication Quality** *(where applicable)* — For open-ended and video responses: clarity, structure, and specificity — not personality.

Each component is weighted based on the role's requirements as configured at job creation. Weights are visible to the hiring team before any candidates take the assessment.

## SECTION 5 — Assessment Integrity

**H2:** Built for integrity, not just functionality

Assessments are only valuable if they measure real performance. QualifyMe makes gaming difficult — and surfaces it when it happens.

- Randomized question ordering within sets
- Per-question time limits
- Browser-activity monitoring during assessments
- Video proctoring with behavioral anomaly detection
- Response pattern analysis to flag non-genuine attempts
- Candidate identity verification at session start

No assessment results are hidden from the hiring team — including integrity flags, so you make the call.

---

## SECTION 6 — Consistency Guarantee

**H2:** Same role. Same bar. Every candidate.

When two candidates apply for the same role, they receive the same question set, the same evaluation rubric, and their scores are computed against the same baseline. No interviewer variance. No resume lottery.

This means:
- You can compare a candidate who applied Monday with one who applied Friday — fairly
- You can reopen a role and hold new applicants to the exact same standard
- Any score can be audited with a full breakdown, visible to hiring managers

## SECTION 7 — Why This Matters

**H2:** Hiring decisions have consequences

A bad hire costs 3–5× the role's salary. The tools you use to make that decision should be designed to make it less likely — not just faster.

Most AI hiring products optimize for speed. QualifyMe optimizes for correctness, then speed.

Our architecture reflects a simple belief: **the quality of a hire should depend on candidate capability — not on who reviewed the resume, or which interviewer showed up that day.**
