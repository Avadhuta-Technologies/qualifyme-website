## SECTION: STAGE 1 — SCREENING

### Anchor
`#stage-1-screening`

### Tag
Stage 1 · Available Now

### Headline
Move fast. Don't miss anyone.

### Subheadline
AI Screening turns a 300-applicant pile into a ranked shortlist — before your first human review.

### The problem this stage solves
When volume is the constraint — not quality — the most expensive thing you can do is spend senior hiring time on initial filtering. Every hour a recruiter or engineering lead spends reviewing unqualified CVs is an hour not spent on the candidates who matter.

Manual screening has two failure modes:
1. **Too slow** — Days spent reviewing applications that could be processed in minutes.
2. **Too blunt** — Keyword-based ATS filtering passes mediocre candidates who know the right terms and eliminates strong ones who don't write for robots.

### How QualifyMe handles Stage 1

**Auto-generate the assessment.** Paste in a job description. QualifyMe builds a role-specific assessment — MCQs, coding challenges, video Q&A, or a combination — matched to the technical and domain requirements of the role. No configuration required. Edit if you want to; approve as-is if the output is right.

**Every applicant completes it.** The assessment runs before any human review. Candidates complete on their own device, at their own pace, within your set window. No scheduling. No coordination overhead.

**AI evaluates and ranks.** Every response is scored automatically against a consistent rubric. You get a ranked candidate list the moment the assessment window closes — or in real time as candidates complete.

**Your team reviews the shortlist.** Not the pile. Recruiters and hiring managers spend their time on the top performers — already ranked, already scored, already explained.

### Stage 1 capabilities
- Role-specific MCQs auto-generated from job description
- Coding challenges across common tech stacks
- Video Q&A with AI response evaluation
- Instant candidate ranking with score breakdowns
- Custom multi-round workflows (e.g. screen → technical round → video)
- Branded candidate portal — candidates see your brand, not ours

### What comes next
Screening tells you who passed. It doesn't tell you who'll thrive. For the roles that matter most, the shortlist from Stage 1 feeds directly into Stage 2.

## SECTION: STAGE 2 — EVALUATION

### Anchor
`#stage-2-evaluation`

### Tag
Stage 2 · Early Access — Launching Soon

### Headline
You have a shortlist.
Now find out who can actually do the job.

### Subheadline
Cognitive Simulations put shortlisted candidates into realistic work scenarios. You see how they think, decide, and perform — before the offer.

### The problem this stage solves
The shortlist from Stage 1 is a list of people who passed a knowledge test. That's valuable — but it's not enough for a hire you'll live with for years.

Interview rounds at this stage introduce new problems: scheduling overhead, inconsistent questions, interviewer bias, and a selection process where the most confident candidate — not the most capable — often advances.

What you need at this stage isn't more conversation. It's performance evidence.

### How QualifyMe handles Stage 2

**Scenario-matched to the role.** The Stage 1 shortlist enters a cognitive simulation generated for the specific job — an SRE candidate faces a production incident, a product manager faces a cross-functional conflict, a sales leader enters a high-stakes negotiation mid-turn.

**Candidates perform — they don't answer.** There's no correct answer to recall. The scenario requires real decisions — with consequences. Every choice the candidate makes changes what they face next. The simulation responds like the actual job does.

**Adaptive difficulty reveals the ceiling.** If the baseline scenario can't separate strong from exceptional, the simulation escalates. A second failure. A tighter constraint. A relationship under pressure. Calibrated to reveal how candidates adapt and recover — not just whether they can handle the baseline.

**Structured output, not a score.** The simulation produces a hiring report: what the candidate did at each decision point, how they handled ambiguity and pressure, where they excelled and where they struggled. Evidence, not inference.

### Stage 2 capabilities
- Cognitive simulation scenarios matched to job description
- Consequence-based decision environments — no scripted correct path
- Adaptive difficulty escalation based on candidate performance
- Full decision trace — every action timestamped and annotated
- Phase-by-phase performance breakdown
- Candidate comparison view (same scenario, multiple candidates)
- Full audit trail — every recommendation reproducible

### Stage 1 → Stage 2 handoff
Candidates who pass the Stage 1 screening threshold can be automatically progressed to a simulation invitation. No manual export. No second setup. The same platform, the next layer.

## SECTION: STAGE 3 — DECISION

### Anchor
`#stage-3-decision`

### Tag
Stage 3 · Combined Insights

### Headline
One recommendation.
Backed by everything you've measured.

### Subheadline
The final hiring decision is the hardest part. QualifyMe gives the hiring team a clear, evidence-based recommendation — not a stack of interview notes to reconcile.

### The problem this stage solves
Final hiring decisions are where bias concentrates. By Stage 3, a shortlist of strong candidates has been filtered down to 2–4 finalists — and the difference between them is often small, ambiguous, and highly dependent on who happened to interview them.

Common failure modes at this stage:
- **Consensus decision-making** — The team defaults to the candidate everyone liked, not the candidate who performed best.
- **Conflicting interview feedback** — Different interviewers weighted different things, and there's no unified framework for comparing them.
- **The gut feel close** — "I just had a good feeling about Candidate C." The decision is made, but it can't be explained or defended if questioned.

### How QualifyMe supports Stage 3

**Combined scoring view.** Stage 1 screening scores + Stage 2 simulation performance are combined in a single candidate comparison view. One number is a shorthand. The report shows what's behind it.

**Hiring recommendation.** The simulation output includes a structured recommendation: Hire / Consider / Pass — with the specific behavioral evidence that supports it. Not an AI opinion. A summary of observed actions.

**Cross-candidate comparison.** Put two or three finalists side by side on the same simulation. Same scenario, same difficulty — direct comparison on the dimensions that matter for the role.

**Audit trail.** Every recommendation is traceable to a specific candidate action. If the decision is questioned — by a rejected candidate, a hiring committee, or an internal review — QualifyMe can show exactly why each candidate ranked where they did.

**Defensible, not just confident.** The goal isn't certainty (hiring never has it). It's confidence grounded in evidence. The hiring team makes a decision they can stand behind — and explain.

### What the Stage 3 output includes
- Combined Stage 1 + Stage 2 candidate scores
- Simulation hiring recommendation per candidate (Hire / Consider / Pass)
- Behavioral evidence summary — key decision points and what they revealed
- Cross-candidate comparison on the same scenario
- Full audit log — timestamped, reproducible, exportable
