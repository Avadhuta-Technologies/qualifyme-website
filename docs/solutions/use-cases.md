## SECTION: USE CASE 1 — HIGH-VOLUME HIRING

### Anchor
`#high-volume`

### Tag
Use Case 01

### Headline
Too many candidates. Not enough signal.

### Body
When a single job posting attracts 200+ applications, the real problem isn't finding good candidates — it's finding them before your team burns out screening everyone else.

Most hiring teams default to one of two bad options: spend days manually reviewing CVs, or rely on keyword filters that eliminate good candidates and pass through bad ones.

QualifyMe replaces both with automated, role-specific assessments that run before any human reviews a single application.

### How it works

- Paste a job description. QualifyMe generates a customized assessment in minutes — MCQs, coding challenges, video Q&A, or a combination.
- Every applicant completes the assessment on their own time, through a branded candidate portal.
- AI evaluates every response instantly. You get a ranked shortlist — not a pile.
- Your team reviews the top performers. No resume sifting. No manual scoring.

### Result
From 300 applicants to a shortlist of 20 in the time it used to take to read the first 30 CVs.

## SECTION: USE CASE 2 — IMPROVING CANDIDATE QUALITY

### Anchor
`#candidate-quality`

### Tag
Use Case 02

### Headline
You're interviewing the right resumes.
You're not interviewing the right people.

### Body
Resume quality and job performance have a weak correlation — and everyone in hiring knows it. Polished CVs, well-rehearsed answers, and confident interview presence tell you who's good at getting hired. They don't tell you who's good at the job.

QualifyMe shifts the selection signal from what candidates claim to what they can actually do.

### How it works

- **AI Screening** evaluates real technical skills — not claimed experience. A candidate who can solve the problem ranks higher than one who lists the right technologies.
- **Cognitive Simulations** go further: candidates are placed in realistic work scenarios and must make actual decisions. Not "how would you handle this" — what do they do when it's in front of them.
- The result is a shortlist built on demonstrated capability — not self-reported background.

### What changes
Before QualifyMe: Hiring managers picking candidates based on resume formatting, school names, and years-of-experience proxies.

After QualifyMe: Hiring managers reviewing candidates ranked by how they actually performed on the skills and decisions the role requires.

## SECTION: USE CASE 3 — HIRING FOR CRITICAL ROLES

### Anchor
`#critical-roles`

### Tag
Use Case 03

### Headline
Some hires you can recover from getting wrong.
Some you can't.

### Body
A bad junior hire costs you onboarding time. A bad senior hire — a lead engineer, a sales director, a product lead — costs you quarters. Sometimes companies. The stakes are different, so the evaluation should be different.

Screening scores and interview impressions aren't enough when the hire needs to lead a team, own a function, or make decisions that compound.

### What critical role hiring requires

- **Depth over speed.** You're not filtering volume — you're deciding between three strong candidates. The evaluation needs to reveal what generic interviews can't.
- **Decision-making under pressure.** How does the candidate perform when the situation is ambiguous, the information is incomplete, and the consequences of a wrong choice are real?
- **Evidence, not impressions.** A defensible hiring recommendation — not a gut feeling that's hard to explain to a stakeholder.

### QualifyMe Cognitive Simulations for critical roles

Candidates enter a job-realistic scenario that reflects the actual cognitive demands of the role. For a VP of Engineering: competing priorities, a team conflict, a technical debt trade-off. For a Sales Director: a negotiation mid-turn with a sophisticated counterparty.

Every action is tracked. Every decision has consequences. The output is a structured hiring recommendation backed by observed behavior — not a number.

## SECTION: USE CASE 4 — REDUCING INTERVIEW BIAS

### Anchor
`#reducing-bias`

### Tag
Use Case 04

### Headline
The best candidate in the room isn't always the best performer on the job.

### Body
Interviews are a known source of hiring bias — structured around first impressions, confidence signals, and cultural familiarity that don't correlate with job performance. The most common biases aren't malicious. They're human: affinity bias (I like how they think), halo effect (strong on one dimension, assumed strong overall), and narrative bias (great story = great performer).

The solution isn't better interviewers. It's less dependence on interviewer judgment at the stages where objective data is available.

### How QualifyMe reduces evaluation bias

**Standardized assessments:** Every candidate for the same role takes the same assessment — same questions, same scenario, same scoring rubric. No variation based on who reviewed the CV or who ran the call.

**AI scoring against defined criteria:** Responses are scored against the same rubric, applied consistently across all candidates. The score reflects performance on the task — not presentation style or interview confidence.

**Cognitive simulations — the same scenario for every candidate:** The same starting conditions, the same adaptive escalation logic, the same evaluation framework. One candidate's SRE simulation is structurally identical to every other SRE simulation. Differences in outcome reflect differences in performance.

**Full audit trail:** Every hiring recommendation is traceable to specific candidate actions. If a hiring decision is questioned, QualifyMe can show exactly why each candidate ranked where they did.

## SECTION: USE CASE 5 — FASTER DECISION MAKING

### Anchor
`#faster-decisions`

### Tag
Use Case 05

### Headline
Hiring decisions that drag are decisions that cost you.

### Body
Every week a role stays open is a week your team is stretched, projects are delayed, or a competitor hires the candidate you're still deciding on. Hiring speed and hiring quality are usually framed as a trade-off. They don't have to be.

The reason hiring decisions drag isn't usually a lack of information — it's too much of the wrong kind. Multiple interview loops, conflicting subjective feedback, and no shared language for comparing candidates.

### QualifyMe compresses the decision cycle

**Before interviews:** Automated assessment scores arrive the same day candidates complete. No waiting for coordinator notes or manual review.

**Entering interviews:** Hiring managers review the assessment report before the interview — already knowing where each candidate is strong and where to probe deeper.

**At decision time:** Instead of "I felt like Candidate B communicated more clearly," teams can point to "Candidate B scored 12 points higher on the analytical reasoning module and made the right escalation call in the simulation where Candidate A guessed."

**Final decisions:** For critical roles, a simulation recommendation gives the hiring team a defensible choice — not a consensus opinion.

### Result
Reduce time-to-decision. Not by rushing — by replacing ambiguous signal with clear evidence.

# IMPLEMENTATION NOTES FOR CODING AGENT

### Page structure notes
- Each use case section should have a clear anchor ID (`#high-volume`, `#candidate-quality`, etc.) for deep linking from other pages
- The hero can include a subtle horizontal nav or jump-link bar beneath the subheadline so visitors can jump directly to the use case relevant to them
- Use case numbers (01–05) should render as a visual tag/badge, not just plain text

### Visual priorities
1. **Use case sections** — Each section should have a consistent visual treatment: tag badge, headline, body copy, how-it-works list, stat bar (where present), CTA. Consider a subtle left border accent or section background alternation to create rhythm without adding noise.
2. **Stat inline blocks** — The 2–3 stat callouts per section should be a mini card row, not a table. Bold number, smaller descriptor. Same style as homepage stats.
3. **Jump nav** — A horizontal sticky sub-nav or anchor pill list below the hero would help visitors self-select their use case without scrolling through all five. Consider: "High Volume · Candidate Quality · Critical Roles · Reducing Bias · Faster Decisions"

### Responsive behavior
- Use case sections: Body + list + stats stack naturally on mobile
- Stats inline blocks: 3 columns → 1 column on mobile
- Jump nav: Horizontal scroll on mobile (or collapse into a dropdown)
- Bottom CTA: Two buttons side by side → stacked on mobile
