### Step 1 — Create your job

**Headline:** Start with a job description. We take it from there.

**Body:**
Paste in your job description — or build one from scratch using the role builder. QualifyMe reads the requirements and extracts the key competencies the role demands: technical skills, domain knowledge, behavioral traits, and cognitive demands.

No configuration required. No question libraries to browse. The platform maps the JD to the right assessment automatically.

**What you do:** Paste or create your JD.
**What QualifyMe does:** Maps role requirements to assessment design.

**Callout:** Supports roles across engineering, product, sales, data, design, operations, and more.

---

### Step 2 — Generate your assessment

**Headline:** A ready-to-send assessment — built from your role, not a template.

**Body:**
The platform generates a complete, customized assessment based on your JD. Depending on your plan and role type, this can include:

- **MCQs** — Domain knowledge, conceptual understanding, job-specific scenarios
- **Coding challenges** — Practical engineering or data problems with auto-validated test cases
- **Video Q&A** — Async interview questions focused on communication, judgment, and fit
- **Cognitive simulation** *(early access)* — Full interactive scenario, generated from the cognitive demands of the role

Review the generated assessment. Edit any question, adjust weights, add your own prompts, or approve the draft as-is. Most assessments are ready to send in under 5 minutes.

**What you do:** Review, adjust (optional), approve.
**What QualifyMe does:** Generates a role-calibrated assessment ready to send.

---

### Step 3 — Candidates complete the assessment

**Headline:** Candidates complete on their own time. You track progress in real time.

**Body:**
Send your assessment via the QualifyMe candidate portal — or integrate with your ATS to trigger automatically. Candidates receive a branded invitation link and complete the assessment on any device, in any timezone, within the window you set.

The candidate experience is designed to feel professional and fair:
- Clean, distraction-free interface
- Clear instructions and time guidance
- Works on desktop, tablet, and mobile
- No downloads, no installs, no friction

You can track completion status live in your dashboard. Automated reminder emails handle follow-up so you don't have to.

**What you do:** Send invites. Monitor completion.
**What QualifyMe does:** Hosts the assessment, handles delivery, sends reminders, tracks status.

---

### Step 4 — AI evaluates every response

**Headline:** Every answer scored. Every candidate ranked. Before you've opened a single result.

**Body:**
As candidates submit, AI evaluates each response in real time using the role-specific rubric generated in Step 2. Scoring is consistent across every candidate — no fatigue, no bias, no variability.

- MCQs scored against validated answer logic and conceptual depth
- Code challenges tested against auto-generated test cases and quality metrics
- Video Q&A analyzed for response quality, structure, and communication clarity
- Simulation responses *(early access)* traced through a decision-consequence model — not sentiment analysis

Every score is traceable to a specific response or action. No black box.

**What you do:** Nothing. Let it run.
**What QualifyMe does:** Scores every response, applies rubric, generates candidate-level breakdowns.

---

### Step 5 — Review your ranked results

**Headline:** Open your dashboard to a shortlist, not a pile.

**Body:**
When evaluation is complete, your dashboard shows every candidate ranked and scored — with full breakdowns by section, question, and competency.

**What you see:**
- Ranked candidate list with overall scores
- Breakdown by assessment section (MCQ / Code / Video)
- Individual question-level responses and scores
- AI-generated summary of each candidate's strengths and gaps
- Flag and shortlist tools for team collaboration

**What you do with it:**
Filter by score. Shortlist the top performers. Share with hiring managers. Move candidates to the next stage — or reject with a single click.

No manual scoring. No spreadsheet tracking. No coordination overhead.

**What you do:** Review, filter, shortlist.
**What QualifyMe does:** Delivers a ranked, explained candidate list with full scoring transparency.

---

### Step 6 — Go deeper with Cognitive Simulations *(optional)*

**Tag:** Early Access — Launching Soon

**Headline:** When screening gives you a shortlist, simulations help you decide.

**Body:**
For the roles that matter most — senior hires, leadership positions, high-stakes decisions — screening tells you who can do the job. Simulations tell you who will excel at it.

After shortlisting from your screening results, run your finalists through a cognitive simulation:

- Each finalist enters the same scenario — a realistic work challenge matched to the role
- Every decision they make has consequences — the scenario responds like the real job
- When we need to see more, the simulation escalates — a second failure, a tighter constraint, a relationship under pressure
- At the end, you receive a structured hiring recommendation for each candidate, backed by their full decision trace

This isn't a second screen — it's a different kind of signal. Not who passed the test. Who'll perform when things get hard.

**What you do:** Decide which finalists to simulate. Review the recommendation reports.
**What QualifyMe does:** Generates the scenario, runs the simulation, produces structured hiring recommendations.

# IMPLEMENTATION NOTES FOR CODING AGENT

### Visual priorities
1. **Step flow** — The 6 steps are the heart of this page. They need a strong visual treatment: numbered steps, clear hierarchy, and ideally a UI callout or illustration per step. Consider a scrolling timeline layout where each step occupies a clear visual space.
2. **"What you don't have to do" table** — Render as a comparison table with clear left/right columns. Not a plain markdown table — consider icon indicators (✗ for old way, ✓ for new way) per row.
3. **Timeline table** — Keep it clean. A simple two-column table or timeline visual. The goal is to defuse anxiety about "how long does this take."
4. **Persona cards** — Three cards for recruiter / hiring manager / HR leader. Consider role-specific icons.

### Scroll behavior
This page is inherently sequential — visitors are reading through a process. Design for progressive disclosure:
- Each step should feel like it "completes" at one viewport
- Use anchor links in a sticky secondary nav (Step 1, Step 2... Step 6) for easy navigation

### Tags
- Step 6 (Simulations): amber "Early access" badge
- After launch: remove tag, update Step 6 copy to remove "optional" framing if simulations become a core workflow

### Step numbering
Steps 1–5 are live product (AI Screening). Step 6 is early access (Simulations). Make this distinction clear visually without downplaying Step 6 — it's a differentiator, not an afterthought.
