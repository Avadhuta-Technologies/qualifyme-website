## SECTION: WHAT ARE COGNITIVE SIMULATIONS?

### Headline
Not a quiz. Not a case study. Something closer to the actual job.

### Body
A cognitive simulation is a structured, interactive work scenario where candidates must make real decisions — with consequences.

It's not a knowledge test. There's no hidden answer key. The scenario is modeled on how the actual job unfolds: incomplete information, competing priorities, stakeholders with different incentives, and outcomes that depend on how the candidate chooses to act.

What we're measuring isn't what they know. It's how they think.

## SECTION: HOW IT WORKS

### Layout
Step-by-step. Left: numbered step with content. Right: UI visual or illustration per step. Alternating layout.

> **Note to dev:** This section is the centerpiece of the page. Each step needs a strong visual — either a real UI mockup or a stylized illustration. If a looping animation is possible, it would significantly lift conversion.

### Headline
How a cognitive simulation works — from job description to hiring decision.

### Steps

**Step 1 — You describe the role. We build the scenario.**
Paste in a job description. QualifyMe analyzes the cognitive demands of the role — crisis management, stakeholder navigation, analytical rigor, creative judgment — and generates a simulation calibrated to what the job actually requires.

No generic templates. Every simulation is matched to the specific thinking demands of the position.

**Step 2 — The candidate enters a realistic workspace.**
They see a simulated work environment: dashboards, documents, communications, and a problem to solve. An SRE candidate faces a system degrading under load. A product manager walks into conflicting stakeholder demands. A sales leader enters a high-stakes negotiation mid-turn.

It feels like real work because it's modeled on how real work unfolds — not what a test-writer imagined it might feel like.

**Step 3 — Every decision has consequences.**
The simulation responds to what the candidate does — not just what they say.

Rush a fix without investigating? The system might get worse. Overcommit to a stakeholder? That promise compounds into the next phase. Avoid a difficult conversation? The tension surfaces later, at higher cost.

There's no correct answer to memorize. Only choices, and what happens because of them.

**Step 4 — The challenge adapts when we need to see more.**
If the baseline scenario can't separate strong from exceptional, the simulation escalates. A second failure hits. A constraint tightens. A relationship breaks down under the added pressure.

Not randomized difficulty — calibrated escalation, designed to reveal how candidates adapt and recover when their first approach stops working.

**Step 5 — You get a clear recommendation — not a vague score.**
When the simulation ends, you receive a structured hiring report:

- What the candidate did at each decision point
- Whether they investigated before acting, or guessed under pressure
- How they handled ambiguity, conflict, and escalation
- Where they excelled and where they struggled
- A hiring recommendation mapped to specific evidence — not a number

## SECTION: KEY CAPABILITIES

### Headline
What makes cognitive simulations different.

### Layout
Three feature blocks — full-width, with icons or illustrations

---

**Capability 1: Real scenarios, not hypotheticals**

Most assessments ask what candidates *would* do. Simulations ask them to *actually do it*.

Scenario design is based on real role archetypes — drawn from the cognitive and behavioral demands that distinguish strong performers in each function. Every challenge reflects how real work actually unfolds: ambiguous, multi-threaded, and dependent on judgment.

*Result: You see decision-making under realistic conditions — not self-reported behavior.*

---

**Capability 2: Consequence-based decisions**

The simulation tracks every action and feeds forward. What you do in phase one changes what you face in phase two.

This eliminates the ability to game the system with "correct" answers. There is no single correct path — only the logic of decisions compounding, the way they do in actual jobs.

*Result: You see how candidates reason, adapt, and take ownership — not just what they think you want to hear.*

---

**Capability 3: Adaptive difficulty**

The simulation engine monitors candidate performance in real time. When a candidate is performing well, the system can introduce additional complexity — a second failure, a tighter constraint, a relationship under pressure.

When a candidate is struggling, the system stays the course — ensuring a fair, consistent baseline for comparison.

*Result: The simulation reveals the ceiling of each candidate's capability — not just whether they crossed a threshold.*

## SECTION: EXAMPLE SCENARIOS

### Headline
Your role. Your cognitive demands. Your simulation.

### Body
Every simulation is generated from the specific job description you provide. Here are examples of the kinds of scenarios QualifyMe builds.

### Cards *(2-column grid)*

---

**Hiring an SRE?**
The scenario: A production system is degrading under load. Latency is rising, stakeholders are asking questions, and every intervention carries risk.

*We're evaluating:* Triage discipline — do they investigate the root cause or patch under pressure? Escalation judgment — when do they pull in help vs. own the fix? Communication under load — how do they manage stakeholder expectations during an active incident?

---

**Hiring a Product Manager?**
The scenario: Sprint planning. Engineering, design, and leadership are pulling in different directions. Scope is creeping, timelines are slipping, and someone needs to say no without burning trust.

*We're evaluating:* Boundary-setting — can they protect scope without alienating stakeholders? Conflict navigation — how do they handle competing priorities without defaulting to appeasement? Commitment management — are the timelines they set realistic and defensible?

---

**Hiring a Sales Leader?**
The scenario: Mid-negotiation. The deal is valuable, but the counterparty has leverage. Time pressure is real. Every turn changes the dynamic.

*We're evaluating:* Anchoring strategy — when do they move vs. hold? Rapport management — how do they maintain the relationship while protecting their position? Closing instinct — do they know when the deal is ready to close?

---

**Hiring a Data Analyst?**
The scenario: A noisy dataset, a tight deadline, and a compelling pattern — that might not be real.

*We're evaluating:* Hypothesis discipline — do they resist the urge to conclude too early? False-positive awareness — can they identify when a signal is noise? Analytical rigor — do they document assumptions and communicate uncertainty?

---

**Hiring a Backend Engineer?**
The scenario: A system drowning in technical debt. Limited resources, a shrinking budget, and every optimization has a downstream cost.

*We're evaluating:* Trade-off analysis — do they measure before they optimize? Refactoring courage — can they make the hard call to rework rather than patch? Dependency awareness — do they understand the systemic effects of their choices?

---

**Hiring a Designer?**
The scenario: An open brief with real constraints — budget, timeline, audience fit. Do they explore boldly or converge too early? Can they kill their darlings when the data says to?

*We're evaluating:* Divergent thinking — do they generate options before committing? Iteration discipline — do they refine, or do they fall in love with the first idea? Convergence judgment — can they recognize when exploration needs to end?

## SECTION: OUTPUT & REPORTS

### Headline
Not a score. A story — with evidence.

### Body
A simulation score tells you very little. A simulation report tells you everything.

After every simulation, QualifyMe generates a structured hiring recommendation document. Every finding maps to a specific action the candidate took — not an inference, not a prediction, an observed behavior.

### Report Features *(checklist or icon list)*

- **Decision trace** — Every action the candidate took, timestamped and annotated
- **Phase-by-phase breakdown** — How they performed at each stage of the scenario
- **Strength and gap summary** — Clear articulation of where they excelled and where they fell short
- **Escalation response** — How they handled the adaptive challenge (if triggered)
- **Hiring recommendation** — Hire / Consider / Pass, with supporting rationale
- **Comparator view** — Side-by-side comparison across multiple candidates on the same scenario
- **Full audit trail** — Every recommendation reproducible and defensible

### Trust Block *(highlighted card)*
**Same candidate. Same scenario. Same result. Every time.**
Scoring is deterministic — not AI-inferred. No hallucinations. No subjective rubrics. Every finding is traceable to a specific action in the simulation.
